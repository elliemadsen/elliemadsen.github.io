In HIAA 0100: Architecture Design Studio, a course at Brown University, my final project was to design and model a house for a monk to live in on a 20 x 20' plot of land in Providence, RI. I chose to implement features that encourage material aspects of monastic living: movements of labor, minimalism, sustainable living, and privacy. The house is set up in a four-level spatial plan and includes an underground cellar, wood-burning stove, high windows, meditation space, and thick adobe walls.
