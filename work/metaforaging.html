metaforaging

poetry

To forage is to explore and gather that which supports and sustains you, without depleting the nuanced systems that provide them. In Metaforaging, a language arts workshop at Brown, I moved through multi-disciplinary practices to understand how our worlds and senses extend and entangle each other. I wrote the following poems for this workshop as I foraged for experiences and objects in my environment, drawing materials from personal and natural sources.

The images are of an experimental observation of decay, composed of plant leaves (strelitzia, epipremnum, philodendron, monstera), fabric, and thread, and constructed via hand-stitching and machine-stitching.
